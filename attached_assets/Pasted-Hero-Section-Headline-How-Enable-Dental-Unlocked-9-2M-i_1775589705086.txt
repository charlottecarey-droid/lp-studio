The Challenge

Enable Dental is a rapidly expanding, 100% mobile DSO based in Texas. Their team brings professional dental care directly to patients' homes and care facilities — serving seniors, homebound individuals, and patients with medical complexities across 16 hubs in 8 states.

With 6,000 annual denture cases, traditional analog workflows were creating a significant bottleneck. Each denture required several in-person appointments — a particular challenge for a mobile operation where every extra visit means additional travel, setup, and coordination time.

The operational burden wasn't just about chair time. For a mobile practice serving vulnerable populations, every unnecessary appointment represented added strain on patients, caregivers, and clinical teams alike.

The Solution

Enable Dental partnered with Dandy to pilot a fully digital denture workflow across their mobile operations. Dandy's end-to-end digital lab platform replaced the traditional analog process with a streamlined digital approach.

With Dandy's intraoral scanners and chairside software deployed to their mobile units, Enable Dental's clinicians could capture precise digital impressions on-site — eliminating the need for messy physical impressions and reducing the number of patient visits required per denture.

The digital workflow enabled clinicians to identify issues early in the process, often eliminating try-in appointments entirely. Combined with Dandy's clinical support team and fast lab turnaround, the pilot demonstrated dramatic efficiency gains from day one.

Why It Matters
For a 100% mobile operation, the efficiency gains compound dramatically. Every eliminated appointment doesn't just save chair time — it saves travel, setup, teardown, and coordination across Enable Dental's mobile units operating in 8 states.

The $9.2M+ in total annualized value represents the combined impact of $2.5M in direct cost savings and $6.7M in production opportunity — value that scales as Enable Dental continues to grow.

For the patients Enable Dental serves — many of whom are elderly, homebound, or living with disabilities — fewer appointments also means less disruption, less caregiver burden, and a meaningfully better care experience.
